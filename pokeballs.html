What are PokeBalls?

Poké Balls are specialized, spherical devices in the Pokémon franchise used to catch, carry, and store Pokémon by converting them into energy.
They allow trainers to transport large creatures easily, with the devices functioning as both storage and a, often comfortable, habitat.


Key Aspects of Poké Balls:

  • Function: Upon hitting a Pokémon, the ball opens and converts the creature into energy, storing it inside.
    Captured Pokémon become loyal to the trainer, though they still possess free will.
  • Types: There are 5 types of balls in Pokemon gen 1, such as the basic Poké Ball,
    the stronger Great Ball and Ultra Ball,Master Ball (guaranteed catch) and Safari Ball Used exclusively in the Safari Zone, with a catch rate similar to an Ultra Ball.

While they generally work by shrinking Pokémon into a pocket-sized form,
the lore sometimes suggests they create a comfortable, specialized, or even, in some interpretations, a data-driven, virtual environment for the creature inside.
