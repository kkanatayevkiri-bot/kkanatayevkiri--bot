What are Items and what are they used for?

In the original Pokémon Red and Blue games (released in the late 90s), items are objects you carry in your inventory to help you catch Pokémon, heal your team, or progress through the story
Since these were the first games in the series, the item system was much simpler than it is today, but it also had some famous limitations. Here is a breakdown of what items are and how they work

Item Categories:

Items in Red and Blue generally fall into four main functional groups:

  1. Poké Balls:
    Used during battle to capture wild Pokémon.
    These include the standard Poké Ball, Great Ball, Ultra Ball, and the one-of-a-kind Master Ball, which never misses.
  2. Medicine & Recovery:
    Items like Potions (restore HP), Antidotes (cure poison), and Ethers (restore move PP).
    These are essential for keeping your team alive during long treks through caves or forests.
  3. Key Items:
    Unique, story-important objects that you cannot sell or throw away.
    Examples include the Bicycle (for faster travel), the S.S.
    Ticket (to board the ship), and the Silph Scope (to see ghosts).
  4. Evolution Stones:
    Special items like the Fire Stone or Water Stone used to force certain Pokémon (like Eevee or Pikachu) to evolve instantly.
